Game Design

Royal Raid

During my final year at DeSales University, I created a game called “Royal Raid.” This was my Senior Capstone project. In the game, the player is a rogue knight fighting against the army of a corrupt king. The objective of the game is to go to the king’s castle and defeat him. There are a certain number of enemies on each map that the player has to defeat. Once a player defeats the required number of enemies (or more), the player can proceed to the next level. The game ends with a boss fight, and once the player defeats the boss (the king), the player wins. I created this game using Autodesk 3ds Max and Unreal Engine 4.

Escape Room

The Escape Room project was done in Unreal Engine 4, and was a class project in an "Advanced Game Development" course. This class was my first time using Unreal Engine 4. In this project, my classmates and I were required to design our separate escape rooms and then combine them. My escape room consisted of a room with many hallways. One of them leads to the player escaping the escape room. The player has to use clues provided throughout the escape room and escape. Some of the hallways are locked by combination locks and locks that require a key. The keys and locks are colored, which lets the player know what the right key for a certain lock. Clues to combinations are provided in the escape room.

Man Hunt (1st Person Shooter)

This game, called "Man Hunt", was created in Unreal Engine 4, just like the escape room, in the same "Advanced Game Development" course. Unlike the Escape Room project, this was an individual project. This game is a first person shooter, and the objective of the game is to eliminate five targets (soldiers) without being in their field of view. There are also platforms to which the player can go to, and elevators which take the player there. Some of the targets are on the platforms, and the player has to be extra careful with them, as their fields of view are not only on the platform. When the player goes into one of the targets' field of view, the player loses one life. The player has three lives to start with. If the player loses all three lives, the player loses. If the player is able to eliminate all the targets without losing all of his/her lives, the player wins.

Animated Twitter Infographic

This project was done in a Digital Art course. In this course, the students were given a list of articles to choose from. I chose an article about Twitter. We were required to pick facts from the article and create an animated infographic about them. This project consisted of three steps:

  1. Design Brief, Script, and Storyboard - An overview and plan of the project
  2. Asset Design - Assets/Icons to use in the final project
  3. Final Animated Infographic - The final project itself

This infographic also contains a voice-over done by a Theater Major at DeSales University. The storyboard and asset designs were all created in Adobe Illustrator CC. The infographic itself was animated in Adobe After Effects CC.
