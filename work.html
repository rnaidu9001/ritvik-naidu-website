Game Design

Royal Raid

This was an individual class project. In this game, the player is a rogue knight fighting against the army of a corrupt king. The objective of the game is to enter the king’s castle and defeat him. There are different levels in this game and several enemies on each level. The player must fight and kill the enemies on each level. The game's finale is a fight with the boss i.e., the King. The player wins only upon defeating the king. I created this game using Autodesk 3ds Max and Unreal Engine 4.

Escape Room

This was a group project as well as an individual project and was created using Unreal Engine 4. In this project, my classmates and I were required to design our separate escape rooms and then combine them. My escape room consisted of a room with many hallways. One of the hallways led to an exit to be able to escape from my escape room. The player must use clues provided throughout the escape room and find his/her escape route. Some of the hallways are locked by combination locks or by locks that require a key. The keys and locks are color coded letting the player figure out the appropriate key for a given lock. In the case of combination locks, the clues are provided in the escape room.

Man Hunt (1st Person Shooter)

This was an individual class project and was created using Unreal Engine 4. This game is a first-person shooter, and the objective is to eliminate five targets (soldiers) without being in their field of view. There are some platforms and elevators to take the player to these platforms. Some of the targets are on the platforms, and the player must use caution here as their fields of view are wider and not restricted to the platforms. When the player enters a target's field of view, the player loses one life. The player has three lives to begin with. If the player loses all three lives, the player loses. If the player eliminates/kills all targets without losing all three lives, the player wins.

Animated Infographic about Twitter

This was an individual class project. The students were given a list of articles to choose from. I chose an article about Twitter. We were required to pick facts from the article and create an animated infographic about it. This project consisted of three steps:

  1. Design Brief, Script, and Storyboard - An overview and plan of the project
  2. Asset Design - Assets/Icons to use in the final project
  3. Final Animated Infographic - The final project itself

This infographic also contains a voice-over given by a student majoring in Theater at DeSales University. The storyboard and asset designs were all created in Adobe Illustrator CC. The infographic itself was animated in Adobe After Effects CC.
